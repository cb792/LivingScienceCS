Early History

The earliest form of ice cream was before 400BC.
People in the Persian Empire would pour grapejuice over snow.

It was served to royality during the summer.

This special chilled food was made of rose water and vermicelli.

Saffron, fruits, and a few other flavors were mized with the ice.

The ice was kept in underground chambers called "yakchal"

In China, around 200 BC, there's evidence of a frozen milk and rice concotion being used.

The Emperor Nero had ice brought down from the mountains to eat with varius fruit toppings in 37-68AD.

It's claimed that a Chinese emperor Kublai Khan kept it a royal secret until Marco Polo visited China.

In the 1500s, Mughal emperors brought ice to Delhi from the Hindu Kush, where they used it in fruit sorbets.

Mr. Polo, Marco, took the technique of making ice cream back to Italy.

I think it was popular because it was super tasty!

European History

Chefs under the Italian Duchess Catherin de' Medici are said to have recipies for sobet or flavored ices arond 1530.

The first French recipe appeared in 1674, and was published in 1694.

The first recipi explicitly for ice cream appeared in London, 1718.

The first referance to ice cream is given by the Oxford English Dictionary , in 1744.
